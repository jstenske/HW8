About Joe

As a finance and business analytics major, my coursework provides a strong foundation for me to showcase my accomplishments in scholarship and experiential learning. I have achieved Dean’s List status every semester before the COVID-19 pandemic began, and I completed the finance major with a 4.00 major GPA. Through my commitment to scholarship, I have been motivated to pursue experiential learning opportunities. One example is my participation as an equity research analyst for the Krause Fund in Fall 2019. I excelled in conducting industry and company research in the Consumer Discretionary Sector and building financial models for this 585K student endowment fund. Additionally, I learned about Tippie’s China study abroad program through Tippie International Buddies, and I was able to participate in the program in May 2018. The study abroad program opened my global aperture by allowing me to meet with eBay and other companies to learn about China’s economy and the regulatory considerations for doing business in China. My experience studying abroad motivated me to encourage other Tippie students to study abroad. These experiences that Tippie afforded me led to my two internships as a Fund Operations Intern for Principal Global Investors (PGI), and a Middle Market Banking Analyst Intern at Wells Fargo. In summer 2019, I developed a liquidity risk management monitoring and governance program to ensure compliance with the Mutual Fund SEC Liquidity Rule for senior management and the Mutual Fund Board at PGI. In summer 2020, I analyzed financial statements and forecasted base and worst-case scenario cash flows during COVID-19 at Wells Fargo. My experience as a summer intern at Wells Fargo and as an equity research analyst for the Krause Fund has emphasized my passion to help companies find value. In the future I hope to be in a position to perform credit evaluation for a large financial institution. I also plan on completing MBA or Master of Finance program as I gain more financial experience.
